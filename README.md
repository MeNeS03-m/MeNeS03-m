### üîê **Networking, Security, and Pentesting Labs**
Hands-on projects where I design and analyze network environments with a focus on security, resilience, and controlled attack simulation.  
I build isolated lab setups to study real-world traffic behavior, identify weaknesses, and apply defensive and offensive techniques in a safe environment.

My work includes:
- Creating segmented network topologies for testing attack paths and lateral movement  
- Deploying honeypots to observe scanning patterns and malicious behavior  
- Hardening Linux hosts through rule sets, service isolation, logging strategies, and least-privilege principles  
- Inspecting packets and protocols to understand how vulnerabilities are exploited in practice  
- Performing controlled tests such as port enumeration, service fingerprinting, and misconfiguration analysis  

I enjoy approaching problems like both a defender and an attacker, because it gives a deeper understanding of how systems fail and how to build them stronger. This reflects my interest in cybersecurity, network engineering, and the mindset needed to secure modern infrastructure.
